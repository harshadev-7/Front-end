Planet Earth Planet Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet in the universe to harbor life. It is the fifth largest planet in the solar system and is unique in that it has a liquid surface water, which is essential for life. Earth's surface is covered by oceans, landmasses, and diverse ecosystems, including forests, deserts, and mountains. The planet's atmosphere is composed primarily of nitrogen and oxygen, with water vapor present, which helps to maintain the planet's temperature and supports life. Earth's rotation and tilt create the seasons, with the equator bulging slightly due to the effects of gravity. The planet's name is derived from Old English and Germanic words meaning "the ground," and it is the only planet in the solar system not named after Greek or Roman mythology.
